# Canonical Schema

The canonical schema represents one official trade-license record after source-specific normalization.

It includes:

- source ID, source URL, source type, fetched time, source freshness, caveats, and redistribution status
- U.S. jurisdiction fields for state, county, and municipality
- agency name and URL
- license number, normalized number, alternate numbers, type, classifications, and trade categories
- business name, DBA name, licensee name, and personnel
- raw status codes and normalized status
- issue, original licensure, effective, expiration, and renewal dates
- public-record address and contact fields
- optional compliance placeholders for bond, insurance, workers' compensation, complaints, and discipline
- raw source record and stable fingerprint

Canonical records do not decide whether a person or business should be trusted. They preserve public-source facts and caveats for downstream use.
